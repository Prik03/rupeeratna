1. What is a Credit Card?

How do credit cards work?

A credit card is a type of payment card that allows customers to pay a merchant for products and services. It provides the cardholder with a line of credit from which they can borrow money to pay for purchases or receive a cash advance. Consumer and business credit cards are the two types of credit cards. Most cards are made of plastic, but some have gemstones embedded in them.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

With a debit card, you can take money out of your bank account without any intermediaries. A credit card allows you to make payments and cash withdrawals and is comparable to a short-term loan from the card provider. Your credit card usage is tallied into a single statement at the conclusion of the billing cycle rather than being repaid on a per-use basis.

Similar to conventional credit cards, secured credit cards operate in the same way. The main distinction between a regular credit card and a secured credit card is that the latter is granted in order to lower risk for card issuers and is secured by collateral, such as fixed deposits. People with no credit history or bad credit history can benefit from secured credit cards. As a result, it is one of the simplest ways to raise your credit score while still making use of credit card benefits. Credit card issuers take less risk by issuing secured credit cards in exchange for a predetermined deposit. The credit card issuer may remove the amount from your deposit if you don't make your payment on time.

Credit cards can be broadly divided into two categories:
  • Standard Credit Cards – Plain credit cards with basic features and no annual fees.
  • Specialized Credit Cards – Credit cards are made specifically to satisfy the cardholder's unique needs. These cards grant a variety of benefits for travel, dining, and other activities.

  • General Cards – Credit cards offer basic features and have relatively low annual fees. These cards are ideal for everyday usage.
  • Reward Points Cards – Every purchase made using a credit card earns the cardholder reward points. A cardholder earns more reward points the more they spend. Gift cards, coupons, and other items can be obtained by exchanging these reward points.
  • Cash-back Credit Cards – Cash-back credit cards provide cash in contrast to reward credit cards, which provide reward points. When a cardholder uses a cash-back credit card, a certain proportion of their purchases are credited back to their account. Periodically, the card account is adjusted to reflect the total cash back earned.

Following receipt of your properly filled application form by the bank, your application will be processed within two weeks. The eligibility requirements listed on the bank's website will let you know if you meet the requirements for a credit card. To find out if you qualify, you can also get in touch with the credit card companies' customer service division.

The maximum credit line granted to you on your credit card is known as a credit limit. The repayment capacity, CIBIL credit report, applicant's profile, and other elements all play a role in determining the credit limit. Nevertheless, you might ask your supplier for an increase in your credit limit. After you submit the request, your provider may require more supporting documentation or carefully review it in light of your financial situation, credit score, and other considerations. Your credit limit will be raised if you are determined to be eligible. Additionally, your provider might boost your annual credit limit if you have paid your credit card bill in whole and on time.
